A brief history of frybread

Frybread is a common and extremely popular dish for many Native American tribes. This staple was developed by the Navajo in the 1800's during 4 years of captivity.


Meager rations of lard, flour, baking soda, sugar, and powdered milk were used in this time to conceptualize this recipe.


Today, nearly every tribe has its own version of frybread. It is a staple to Native American feasts and ceremonies and is a common treat at carnivals and fairs.
