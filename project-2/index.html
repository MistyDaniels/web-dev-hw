Lakota Frybread

Frybread is incredibly easy to make and enjoyed by most, whether youre enjoying it with cinnamon and honey or making Indian Tacos, this basic recipe will get you by.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup of milk*
  • 3/4 cup sugar**
  • 3 cups of vegetable oil

*Milk can be substituted with room temperature water.

**Sugar can be substituted with half a cup of honey.

Directions

  1. Preheat oil in a castiron pan on medium-high heat.
  2. In a large bowl mix flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a seperate bowl combine milk and sugar.
  4. Combine wet and dry ingredients well.
  5. Gently knead until well combined and forming a ball. You may have to add milk or flour as needed.
  6. Dust with a handful of flour and roll out into a 1/2 inch sheet
  7. Use a bowl or cup to cut out frybread shapes.
  8. Cut a small slit in side of dough to allow even cooking.
  9. When oil is hot, place one or two pieces in the pan. Dough will sink and slowly rise as it cooks.
  10. When dough has risen and started to brown, flip it.
  11. When both sides are golden brown, remove and drain on paper towels.
