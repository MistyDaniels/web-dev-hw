A brief history of frybread

Frybread is a common and extremely popular dish for many Native American tribes. This staple was developed by the Navajo in the 1800's during 4 years of captivity by the US government.

The Navajo were provided with meager rations of lard, flour, baking soda, sugar, and powdered milk. These few items let to conceptualize this recipe.

Today, nearly every tribe has its own version of frybread, with every family claiming the best recipe. It is a staple to Native American feasts and ceremonies and is a common treat at carnivals and fairs.
